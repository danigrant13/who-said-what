Title of research study: Person Perception and Decision Making

IRB Protocol Number: IRB Protocol Number: 19-0819

Investigator: Eric Pedersen

Purpose of the Study

The purpose of this study is to understand how people make financial decisions in a social context. This research will help researchers better understand human decision-making. We expect that you will be in this research study for less than one hour. There will be approximately 3,000 total subjects in the study.

Explanation of Procedures

If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked to complete an online survey/questionnaire. This survey/questionnaire will ask about your beliefs, background, and feelings. You may be shown words and pictures. You will be asked to answer questions and make judgments about those stimuli. All of the data collected in this study will come from these measures. This research will take place both online and in Muenzinger Hall, Department of Psychology and Neuroscience at the University of Colorado Boulder. This research will be conducted between May 1, 2020, and August 31, 2023. Your participation will last one hour or less.

Voluntary Participation and Withdrawal

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you can withdraw at any time by simply exiting the survey. Choosing not to participate or withdrawing will result in no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are entitled. You are free to skip any question that you choose. The alternatives to participating in this study are to simply not participate. If you decide to withdraw for the study, any data collected during your session will be erased and will not be used for analyses. Whether or not you take part in this research is your choice. You can leave the research at any time and it will not be held against you. If you would like to learn about the research results, please refer to the experimenter’s contact information.

Risks and Discomforts

There is minimal risk associated with this research study. Risks involved in participating in this study include feeling fatigue, frustration, or boredom.

Potential Benefits

We cannot promise any benefits other than monetary compensation (see below) to you or others from your taking part in this research. Should you have any question regarding your compensation at any time during the experiment, please refer to the experimenter’s contact information.

Confidentiality

Information obtained about you for this study will be kept confidential to the extent allowed by law. Research information that identifies you may be shared with the University of Colorado Boulder Institutional Review Board (IRB) and others who are responsible for ensuring compliance with laws and regulations related to research, including people on behalf of the Office for Human Research Protections. The information from this research may be published for scientific purposes; however, your identity will not be given out.

Payment for Participation

Participation will take between 10 minutes and one hour. You will receive between $5 and $20 for participating in accordance to what was posted on the website reached this study through. Should you have any questions regarding your compensation at any time during the experiment, please refer to the experimenter’s contact information.
